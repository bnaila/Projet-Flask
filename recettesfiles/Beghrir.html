Berghrir

Ingredients

1 tea glass of 150 ml
2 tea glasses of fine semolina
1 tea cup of flour
3 tea glasses of lukewarm water
1 tbsp active dry yeast
1 packet of baking powder
1 tsp salt

Preparation

In a bowl mix the semolina, flour and salt. Add the baker's yeast (active dry yeast for me) and warm (not hot) water.
Mix everything and pour into a blender, mix for about 5 minutes (you have to stop the mixer from time to time so that it does not heat up too much). Add the baking powder and mix a second time for a few minutes (you will see bubbles appear on the surface of the baghrir dough).
Pour into a bowl and let stand for about 20-30 minutes. Heat a pan (I use a thick-bottomed tefal pan) and pour a ladle of baghrir batter. The paste will spread over the surface and will begin to perforate all over the surface bubbles will form and burst. Baghrir are cooked on one side only, unlike pancakes, when there is no more liquid left on the surface, remove the thousand-hole pancake from the pan and place on a clean cloth.
butter the surface of the baghrir, immerse the bottom of the pan in a basin of cold water and put back on the heat, taking care to wipe the surface of the pan before pouring the second ladle. Continue like this until the dough is used up. Serve the baghrir drizzled with a hot butter-honey mixture.
