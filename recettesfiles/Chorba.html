Chorba

Ingredients

5 fresh and ripe tomatoes
1 chopped onion.
1 small zucchini
1 small potato
1 stalk of celery with its leaves (optional)
1 small carrot replacing the peas that I don't have
5 chopped fresh mint leaves
1 bunch of chopped cilantro
salt, black pepper
1 tsp paprika
1 pinch of cinnamon
1 tbsp tomato puree
Â½ glass of Orzo or vermicelli pasta
1 small bowl of chickpeas soaked the day before>

Preparation

Pheat 1 tbsp of oil in a pot and add the onion. Add the vegetables followed by the spices and herbs (cilantro and parsley and the celery stalk and its leaves). Leave on medium heat for a few minutes (about fifteen minutes).
Add the pureed tomatoes and the chickpeas, dilute the tomato concentrate in half a glass of warm water and pour into the pot. Let simmer for 15 minutes over medium heat to allow the flavors to blend. Pour 1.5 L of water, cover the pot or close the casserole and cook for about 15 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
At this stage, you can remove the vegetables (reserve the chickpeas), pass them through a grinder and put them back in the pot. Bring to the boil and pour in the Orzo pasta or vermicelli, mix well with a wooden spoon so that the pasta does not stick to the bottom of the pot.
Cover and cook for a few minutes (Orzo pasta takes longer to cook compared to vermicelli). Return the chickpeas. Adjust the seasoning and serve the chorba accompanied by a lemon wedge and sprinkled with chopped coriander and dried mint.
