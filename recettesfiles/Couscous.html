vegetarian Couscous

Ingredients

500 g fine couscous
100 g raisins
4 tomatoes
2 onions
4 carrots
3 zucchini
4 small turnips
1 bunch of parsley
1 pinch of saffron pistils
½ teaspoon of pepper
1 teaspoon of ginger
1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
Salt according to your taste
Olive oil
50g butter

Preparation of vegetables

Peel the tomatoes (it's more digestible!) and cut them into cubes; Peel the onions, rinse them with water then chop them finely; Peel 5 carrots, wash them, then cut them into strips; Peel 3 courgettes that you wash, and cut them into strips; Peel the 4 small turnips, rinse them; Wash your parsley, then form a bouquet garni using a food thread; In a pot, sauté 4 tablespoons of olive oil, the tomatoes, the onion, the spices (saffron, pepper, ginger, cinnamon) over high heat, then salt to taste for 10 minutes. (Stir occasionally) ; Add 1.5 liters of water to your pot, then leave to simmer covered, cook for 30 minutes after boiling over medium heat; Then add your carrots, cook over medium heat for 5 minutes after boiling; Then add your turnips, the bouquet garni, then simmer over medium heat for 10 minutes after boiling; Then add your zucchini, add 1 liter of water, then cook over medium heat (after boiling) count 15 minutes of cooking and it's ready!

Preparing semolina with raisins (steamed)

Note: For the semolina there is a faster way to cook your semolina (the recipe you find in the couscous box), personally I prefer the traditional cooking method (longer) but the semolina is more airy, and it digests better.
1st stage
For the semolina, the raisins, then 3 tablespoons of olive oil into a large container, then gradually pour in, mixing with a wooden spoon, the equivalent of 25 cl of warm salted water. , then grind the semolina by hand (as if you were rubbing your hands) this avoids the formation of lumps; Cook for 20 minutes;
2nd stage
After 20 minutes pour the semolina into your container and start by shelling your semolina with a wooden spoon, then pour another 25 cl of cold water gradually, then shell the semolina by hand when it has cooled down ; Steam for another 15 minutes and repeat phase 2 once more; To finish, pour the semolina into your container, add 50 grams of butter that you let melt on your semolina, fluff it when it has cooled down and your semolina is ready!
